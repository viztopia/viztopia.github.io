Doppelgänger

Photography by Maria Baranova

Showcase

MAXlive 2021: The Neuroverse | New York Live Arts on Nov 5, 2021

Doppelgänger is a duet for a solo performer and a live stream of the same performer, co-created with Nuum Collective.

In this piece, performer NiNi Dongnier explores isolation and connection as she dances in real-time with her computer-manipulated doppelgänger with the help of machine learning. It asks fundamental questions about what we see and what we think we see, and illustrates how it in fact does not take two to tango. The piece premiered at New York Live Arts as part of the "MAXlive 2021: The Neuroverse" festival.

Story

A friend once told me
that a true conversation
must by necessity
travel long distances.


To manufacture a dialogue between the self and the self “just a few moments ago,” the latency endemic to streaming technologies is exaggerated and manipulated. To what extent is life and our experience of the passage of time an ongoing conversation with ourselves? Is what I do now merely a response to what I did 2 seconds, minutes, years, decades ago? Or is it the beginning of a new thought?

If a life is a conversation between the current, past and future selves, what happens when you collapse that linear conversation (monologue) and experience it in parallel (dialogue)? Does it become more or less comprehensible? Does such an exchange constitute a true conversation or merely one that is believably true?
